Accessibility Statement

Gardiner Public Schools is committed to providing a website that is accessible to all individuals, regardless of ability or technology. We strive to meet or exceed the standards set forth in the Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) 2.2 Level AA, in accordance with Section 504 and Section 508 of the Rehabilitation Act and Title II of the Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA).

We are actively working to increase the accessibility and usability of our website and in doing so adhere to many of the available standards and guidelines. This Accessibility Plan outlines our strategy for maintaining compliance and equitable access to all users.

Accessibility Goals

  • Meet or exceed WCAG 2.2 Level AA standards across all web content.
  • Ensure compatibility with screen readers, keyboard navigation, and assistive technology.
  • Provide alternative text for all images and meaningful graphics.
  • Make PDFs and downloadable documents accessible and tagged properly.
  • Maintain consistent heading structure and semantic HTML.
  • Ensure responsive and mobile-friendly layouts across all devices.

Ongoing Practices

  • Manual testing with NVDA, VoiceOver, and keyboard-only navigation.
  • Staff training for content creators on accessibility principles.
  • Document accessibility ensured through OCR tagging and heading structure.
  • Closed captions and transcripts provided for videos when applicable.

Roles & Responsibilities

Role Responsibility
Website Administrator Implement and test accessibility in HTML/CSS/JS codebase.
Content Editors Ensure all uploaded content is accessible and readable.
District Technology Director Oversee audits, training, and compliance tracking.
Superintendent Provide leadership and resource allocation for accessibility improvements.

Need Assistance?

If you are experiencing difficulty accessing any part of our website, or if you have any questions or suggestions regarding accessibility, please contact us:

Annual Review

This Accessibility Plan is reviewed annually each spring to reflect changes in guidelines, technology, and district content strategy.

Summary

Gardiner Public Schools believes digital accessibility is a civil right. This plan reflects our commitment to ensuring an inclusive, compliant, and user-friendly digital experience for all members of our community.
