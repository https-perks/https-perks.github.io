Elementary Student Discipline

Discipline Philosophy

Gardiner School’s discipline program emphasizes the following expectations:

  • No student has the right to interfere with another student’s learning.
  • No student has the right to interfere with a teacher’s teaching.
  • No student has the right to create an unsafe environment.

Discipline is primarily the responsibility of the classroom teacher, who must maintain a safe and respectful learning environment. Gardiner Schools utilizes the To Be A Bruin program to teach and model positive, prosocial behavior across the campus to reduce disciplinary infractions.

Bullying and Harassment Statement

Board Policy 3226 affirms that Gardiner School District takes bullying and harassment very seriously. A parent and student report form is available to report bullying, harassment, or intimidation at any time.

Definition of Bullying: Harassment, intimidation, hazing, or threatening/insulting/demeaning physical or verbal gestures or contact, including written, electronic, or other forms of communication that are persistent, severe, or repeated and:

  • Cause physical harm or fear of harm to a student or their property.
  • Create a hostile environment or deny educational opportunities.
  • Substantially disrupt the operation of the school.

This includes retaliation against a victim or witness, or hazing related to athletics or school-sponsored activities.

Reporting Procedures

Students, parents, or staff may report bullying using any of the following methods:

  1. Speak directly to a teacher or administrator. A written form may still be requested.
  2. Fill out the online bullying report form or request a paper copy from the front office.
  3. Reports will be investigated promptly. If confirmed, disciplinary consequences will be applied based on the severity and frequency of behavior.

Consequences Table

Below are the general consequences for confirmed incidents. Administration will consider severity, frequency, context, and student history in final determinations.

Offense Description Potential Consequences
Bullying Repeated, severe hurtful attacks (including cyberbullying) that affect safety and learning. Victim does not reciprocate.
  • 2–10 day OSS (Out-of-School Suspension)
  • Counseling or mediation
  • Parent conference
  • Possible expulsion depending on severity or history
Harassment/Intimidation Verbal or physical actions that demean others based on identity (e.g., race, religion, disability, etc.).
  • 1–10 day OSS
  • Possible expulsion
  • Law enforcement referral
  • Safety plan as needed
Sexual Harassment Unwanted sexual remarks, gestures, or contact that cause humiliation, embarrassment, or intimidation.
  • 1–10 day OSS
  • Possible expulsion
  • Law enforcement referral
  • Safety plan in place
Sexual Assault Non-consensual sexual contact with another student or adult.
  • 1–10 day OSS
  • Possible expulsion
  • Mandatory referral to law enforcement
  • Immediate safety interventions
