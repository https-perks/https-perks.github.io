School Safety

The Gardiner School District is committed to the safety and well-being of all students, staff, and visitors. We partner closely with local law enforcement and emergency services to ensure thorough planning and coordinated responses to potential emergencies.

School Safety & Security

We maintain strong working relationships with Gardiner Emergency Services, Yellowstone National Park Law Enforcement, Park County Health Department, and the Red Cross to develop and implement a comprehensive emergency response plan. In the event of a school emergency, families should:

  • Tune into local television or radio news
  • Check email or cell phone for School Messenger alerts
  • Visit the Gardiner Schools website

Please report any concerns to the school principal or superintendent.

Safety Measures

We implement numerous safety protocols, which include:

  • Police/YNP Drop-Ins: Routine, unannounced visits during the school day
  • Canine Detection: Contracted program using trained detection dogs
  • Emergency Drills: Regular intruder, fire, and disaster drills
  • Safety Audits: Biannual building reviews from our insurance provider
  • Crisis Response Team: Trained staff supporting students and staff during critical events

Building Access & Procedures

  • Visitors must check in and sign in at the main office during school hours
  • All external entrances are locked during instructional hours

Community Partnerships

  • Collaboration with law enforcement, fire, and public health services
  • Ongoing policy review and emergency planning with community partners
  • Partnership with Livingston HealthCare for mental health support

How Parents Can Help

  • Keep emergency contact information up to date
  • Subscribe to School Messenger alerts
  • Talk with children about school safety and emergency procedures

Emergency Communication

In the event of a lockdown or evacuation, parents will be directed to a designated location and must present identification to pick up their child. Students will only be released to adults listed as emergency contacts.

  • Use local news sources and the school website for updates
  • Watch for School Messenger notifications

Ongoing Staff Training

All staff receive annual training in emergency response procedures. Emergency drills are held throughout the year and follow the National Incident Management System (NIMS) framework.

Student Support After a Crisis

Our Crisis Response Team and community mental health providers are available to support students and staff after emergencies. For additional guidance, view the Resource Guide for Parents & Teachers.
