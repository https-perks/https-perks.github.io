School Safety

The Gardiner School District is committed to the safety and well-being of all students, staff, and visitors. We partner closely with local law enforcement and emergency services to ensure thorough planning and coordinated responses to potential emergencies.

School Safety & Security

We maintain strong working relationships with Gardiner Emergency Services, Yellowstone National Park Law Enforcement, Park County Health Department, and the Red Cross to develop and implement a comprehensive emergency response plan. In the event of a school emergency, families should:

  • Tune into local television or radio news
  • Check email or cell phone for School Messenger alerts
  • Visit the Gardiner Schools website

Please report any concerns to the school principal or superintendent.

Safety Measures

We implement numerous safety protocols, which include:

  • Police/YNP Drop-Ins: Local law enforcement make routine, unannounced visits during the school day.
  • Canine Detection: A contracted program with dogs trained to detect substances and weapons.
  • Emergency Drills: Regularly practiced intruder, fire, and natural disaster drills.
  • Safety Audits: Biannual building reviews from our insurance carrier.
  • Crisis Response Team: Trained staff members offer emotional and logistical support during critical incidents.

Building Access & Procedures

  • Visitors must check in and sign in at the main office during school hours.
  • All external entrances are locked during instructional hours.

Community Partnerships

  • Collaboration with law enforcement, fire services, and public health agencies.
  • Regular policy review and crisis response planning with community partners.
  • Partnership with Livingston HealthCare for mental health services.

How Parents Can Help

  • Keep emergency contact information updated.
  • Subscribe to School Messenger for real-time notifications.
  • Talk to your children about emergency procedures and reporting concerns.

Emergency Communication

In the event of a lockdown or evacuation, parents will be directed to a designated location and must provide identification to pick up their child. Students will only be released to adults listed as emergency contacts.

  • Use local news sources and the school website for updates.
  • Watch for School Messenger alerts.

Ongoing Staff Training

All staff receive annual training in emergency response protocols. Emergency drills are conducted throughout the year, and our protocols align with the National Incident Management System (NIMS).

Student Support After a Crisis

Our Crisis Response Team and community mental health partners are available to support students and staff following emergencies or crises. For more information, refer to the Resource Guide for Parents & Teachers.
