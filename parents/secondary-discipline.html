JH–HS Student Discipline

Discipline Policy

Students are expected to comply with district policies, pursue their coursework diligently, and behave respectfully at school, during transit, or at school events. Authority figures include teachers, the principal, and the superintendent.

Students must submit to reasonable disciplinary actions. Failure to do so may result in suspension or expulsion.

Each student is expected to strive for excellence and contribute to a safe, orderly, and clean educational environment.

Rules of Conduct

All students must follow school policy, including at extracurricular events. The table below outlines offenses and a range of actions based on severity and history. Administration will notify parents for serious offenses and may use in-school or out-of-school suspension, restitution, expulsion, or referral to law enforcement.

Bullying, Harassment, and Intimidation

Board Policy 3226 states that Gardiner Schools take reports seriously and will act immediately to investigate. Retaliation or hazing is also considered bullying behavior.

Bullying Definition: Repeated or severe harassment via gestures, speech, physical contact, or digital messages that:

  • Cause harm or fear of harm
  • Limit access to learning or opportunities
  • Disrupt the educational environment

Reporting Procedures

  1. Speak to a teacher or administrator immediately
  2. Submit a paper or online report form
  3. All reports will be investigated with follow-up as needed

Bullying Consequences
