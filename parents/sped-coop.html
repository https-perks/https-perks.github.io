Park County Special Education Cooperative

The Park County Special Education Cooperative (CO-OP) provides shared special education services across several rural Montana school districts, ensuring eligible students receive the support they need to access a Free and Appropriate Public Education (FAPE).

About the Program

Gardiner Public Schools is a member of the Park County Special Education Cooperative. Other participating districts include Gardiner, Arrowhead, Cooke City, and Pine Creek. The Cooperative exists to help member schools pool resources to provide specialized services to students with disabilities.

Each district, through the Cooperative, ensures that all children with disabilities (ages 3â€“21), including those who are homeless, wards of the state, or attending private schools, are identified, located, and evaluated. Services are provided based on eligibility under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA) and Montana Special Education Regulations.

Specialists employed by the Cooperative typically serve students across multiple districts and may include:

  • Special Education Director(s)
  • School Psychologist(s)
  • Speech-Language Pathologist(s)
  • Occupational Therapist(s)
  • Physical Therapist(s)

The Park County Special Education Cooperative is headquartered on the Arrowhead School District campus.
