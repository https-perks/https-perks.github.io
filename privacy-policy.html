Privacy Policy

This policy outlines how Gardiner Public Schools handles student education records and personal information in compliance with FERPA, COPPA, HIPAA, and local board policies.

Family Educational Rights and Privacy Act (FERPA)

Gardiner Public Schools complies with FERPA, which affords parents and students over 18 years of age the right to:

  • Inspect and review the student's education records within 45 days of a request.
  • Request amendment of inaccurate or misleading records.
  • Consent to disclosures of personally identifiable information, except where FERPA authorizes disclosure without consent.
  • File a complaint with the U.S. Department of Education for alleged failures to comply with FERPA requirements.

Directory Information: The District may disclose directory information unless parents/guardians opt out in writing. Directory information includes: student’s name, address, telephone number, date and place of birth, honors and awards, and dates of attendance.

Children's Online Privacy Protection Act (COPPA)

Gardiner Public Schools ensures compliance with COPPA by obtaining parental consent before collecting personal information from children under 13 through online educational services. Third-party online tools used by the District are evaluated for COPPA compliance, and consent may be granted on behalf of parents where appropriate under COPPA guidelines.

Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act (HIPAA)

While education records are generally exempt from HIPAA under FERPA, Gardiner Public Schools protects any health-related information in compliance with both HIPAA and FERPA. Student health records maintained by the school nurse or health officials are part of the education record and protected accordingly.

Board Policy 3600 – Student Records

In accordance with Board Policy 3600:

  • The District shall maintain a permanent and cumulative record for each student.
  • Access to student records is limited to authorized personnel, parents, eligible students, or those with written consent.
  • Parents may request an amendment to records they believe are inaccurate or violate student privacy.
  • Disclosure of student records is logged and records are retained per state and federal guidelines.

The full policy is available in the District Office or downloadable here (PDF).

Your Rights

You have the right to:

  • Know what information the school collects and how it is used.
  • Opt-out of the release of directory information.
  • Request a review or correction of your child's education records.
  • Withdraw consent for digital learning tools that collect personal data.
